(1)  Elizabeth Cady Stanton first met Lucretia Mott in London in 1840, a chance 
encounter that would herald a new era in women's history.  Stanton, an anti-slavery 
activist, and Mott, a Quaker and veteran advocate of social reform, had traveled as part 
of the American delegation to the World Anti-Slavery Convention being held in the city.  
(5) After being denied seating on the grounds of their sex, Stanton and Mott became 
convinced of the need to formally convene and address the condition of women.  Eight 
years would pass before the event that they envisioned would come about. 
  In July 1848, Mott was visiting her sister in Waterloo, New York, and Stanton was 
living in nearby Seneca Falls when they met again at a social gathering.  As discussion
(10) turned to women's rights, Stanton argued that the time had come for women's issues to 
 be laid before the public and that women themselves must shoulder the responsibility.  
Before the afternoon was out, the women had decided to call a convention "to discuss 
the social, civil, and religious condition and rights of women". 
  Stanton was assigned the task of composing a Declaration of Sentiments for the 
(15) convention.  Drawing upon the American Declaration of Independence, she submitted 
 that "all men and women are created equal" and went on to list the "injuries and 
usurpations" suffered by women at the hands of men.  Among the resolutions Stanton 
drafted for the improvement of women's lot was one positing the radical assertion that it 
was a woman's duty to obtain the right to vote.  This resolution shocked even Lucretia 
(20) Mott, who exclaimed, "Why, Lizzie, you will make us ridiculous."  
  The Seneca Falls Convention took place that same summer at the Wesleyan 
Methodist Church in Seneca Falls.  It was publicized only by a small notice placed in 
the Seneca County Courier, and the organizers anticipated a modest affair.  "The 
convention will not be so large as it otherwise might be, owing to the busy time with the  
(25) farmers," Mott told Stanton, "but it will be a beginning."  Nonetheless, a crowd of three 
hundred people, including forty men, came from miles away.  How ironic that at this 
juncture in the history of the women's movement, no woman was willing to preside over 
the proceedings – the task fell to Lucretia Mott's husband.

תרגום הציון לאחוזים
הטבלה לתרגום טווחי ציונים לאחוזים, המופיעה כאן, תסייע לכם להבין את משמעות הציונים שהתקבלו.  הטבלה מחולקת 
ל-17 קטגוריות של טווחי ציונים.  בעבור כל טווח ציונים מוצג אחוז הנבחנים שציונם נמצא מתחתיו, האחוז שבתוכו והאחוז 
שמעליו. לדוגמה, ציון כללי של 518 נמצא בטווח הציונים 524-500.  כ-40% מהנבחנים קיבלו ציון מתחת לטווח זה, כ-7% 
קיבלו ציון בטווח זה, וכ-53% קיבלו ציון מעל טווח זה.
החלוקה לקטגוריות נעשתה לשם ההדגמה בלבד ואינה משקפת מדיניות קבלה של מוסד כלשהו. תרגום הציון לאחוזים 
מתבסס על כלל אוכלוסיית הנבחנים בבחינה הפסיכומטרית בשנים האחרונות.
